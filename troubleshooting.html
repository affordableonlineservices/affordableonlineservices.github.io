COMPUTER Troubleshooting & Maintenance Services

A comprehensive list of computer troubleshooting and maintenance services typically includes both proactive and reactive measures. These services can be performed by an individual or a professional and cover a wide range of hardware, software, and networking issues.

Common Troubleshooting and Repair Services

These services address specific problems that have already occurred.

  • Diagnostic Services:
    • Hardware Diagnostics: Identifying failing or faulty hardware components (e.g., hard drive, RAM, motherboard, power supply).
    • Software Diagnostics: Pinpointing the cause of software crashes, slow performance, or system errors.
  • Virus and Malware Removal:
    • Deep scanning and removal of viruses, spyware, ransomware, and other malicious software.
    • Installing and configuring antivirus and anti-malware software for future protection.
  • Performance Optimization:
    • System Tune-up: Cleaning up temporary files, clearing browser caches, and removing unnecessary programs.
    • Startup Program Management: Disabling non-essential applications that launch at boot to improve startup speed.
    • Disk Defragmentation: Rearranging fragmented data on a hard disk drive (HDD) for faster access. This is not necessary for Solid State Drives (SSDs).
  • Operating System (OS) and Software Repair:
    • Troubleshooting and resolving OS issues, including boot failures and the "Blue Screen of Death" (BSOD).
    • Repairing or reinstalling corrupted operating systems.
    • Troubleshooting and resolving issues with specific applications.
  • Hardware Repair and Replacement:
    • Component Replacement: Installing new or upgraded components like RAM, hard drives (HDD or SSD), graphics cards (GPU), or power supplies (PSU).
    • Laptop Repair: Fixing broken laptop screens, keyboards, or charging ports.
    • Overheating Issues: Cleaning out dust from fans and internal components, and ensuring proper airflow.
  • Data Services:
    • Data Recovery: Attempting to retrieve lost or corrupted data from a failed hard drive or storage device.
    • Data Backup and Transfer: Setting up a reliable backup system and securely transferring data from an old computer to a new one.
  • Network and Connectivity Troubleshooting:
    • Diagnosing and fixing issues with Wi-Fi, Ethernet, and internet connections.
    • Setting up and securing local area networks (LANs).

Regular Maintenance Services

These are proactive services designed to prevent problems before they happen.

  • Software Updates and Patching:
    • Ensuring the operating system, drivers, and applications are up-to-date with the latest security patches and performance enhancements.
  • Physical Cleaning:
    • Dust removal from the computer's interior, fans, and vents to prevent overheating and component damage.
  • Security Audits:
    • Checking firewall settings, password strength, and overall system security.
  • Backup Solutions:
    • Implementing and verifying regular data backups, either to an external drive or a cloud service.
  • General Health Checks:
    • Conducting routine diagnostic scans to monitor the health of the hardware and software.
  • Computer Setup:
    • Setting up new computers, including hardware installation, software configuration, and data transfer.
